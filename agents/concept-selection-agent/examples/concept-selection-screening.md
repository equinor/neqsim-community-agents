# Example: Concept Selection Screening (Public Synthetic Opportunity)

This example uses a public, synthetic opportunity. No confidential data is used.

## Opportunity

- Resource: ~30 GSm³ gas (contingent), early maturity.
- Concepts: (A) subsea tieback to an existing host 25 km away; (B) standalone
  platform.
- Economic basis: gas price 1.5 (currency/Sm³), discount rate 8%, field life 15 yr.
- Fluid (mole fraction): methane 0.86, ethane 0.06, propane 0.03, CO2 0.02,
  water 0.03 (normalized).

## Screening summary (illustrative)

| Driver | Concept A (tieback) | Concept B (standalone) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Resource class | contingent (same) | contingent (same) |
| Indicative CAPEX | lower | higher |
| Discounted value (NPV trend) | higher | lower |
| Energy / emissions | lower (uses host) | higher |
| Step-out (25 km) | within screening range | not applicable |

## Outcome

On this public basis the tieback (Concept A) ranks ahead on cost, value, and
emissions, with step-out within the screening range. Recommendation: run validated
NeqSim field-development and economics studies (production profile, DCF, emissions)
to confirm before any decision.

**Human review required.** This screening does not replace a validated study.
